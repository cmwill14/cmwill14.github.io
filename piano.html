My Story

How It All Started

I started taking piano lessons when I was young, and over the course of more than ten years I've performed in recitals, practiced classical techniques, and explored a wide variety of musical styles. Piano has been a constant in my life through school, sports, and even a two-year mission to Brazil. It's a discipline that teaches patience, precision, and creativity all at once.

What I love most about piano is how I can express myself and become lost in my own world. When I play the piano, I seriously enjoy it, and I often get comments that I may play a bit too loud, but that's all part of the experience for me.

The 88 keys — a universe of sound.

Watch & Listen

Piano Performance

"Waterfall" by Jon Schmidt of The Piano Guys is the piece that made me take piano seriously. When I first heard it, I was blown away — the speed, the energy, the emotion. It made me realize that piano wasn't just a chore or a lesson to get through; it could be something genuinely thrilling. From that moment on, I stopped just practicing and started truly playing. Jon Schmidt wrote this piece when he was just 17 years old, and it went on to become one of the most recognized piano compositions among LDS musicians — a favorite on the pianos at BYU's Wilkinson Student Center for years.
